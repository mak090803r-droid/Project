Manual of Technical Specifications for Autonomous Operation and Integration of Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAS) Systems Chapter I: Systems Engineering and Cell Integration Specifications 1.1 Aerodynamic configuration and Structural Integrity Analysis (01) The purpose of this Regulation is to establish technical design and integration standards for the cell for Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAS) systems operating in complex weather conditions The design of the cell structure must achieve a precise balance between structural rigidity, aerodynamic leafiness and vacuum mass ratio. For fixed-wing drones using long-wings, the design of the main longeron should give priority to high-module carbon fibre composite materials (such as class T700 or T800). This ensures adequate operational rigidity against torsion under extreme manoeuvring loads (+4 5G to -2.0G) [o2] The internal geometric topology of the cell must necessarily take into account electromagnetic compatibility zoning (EMC). High-frequency radio frequency transmitters (such as data link antennas and video transmission modules) shall be physically isolated from high-precision analogue sensors (such as IMU inertial measurement units and barometers). The cell envelope must incorporate braided conductive meshwork or shielding paints in critical areas to mitigate transient electromagnetic interference generated by motor power cables during peak currents. All PER internal supports shall be of topologically optimized design, combining aeronautical grade Intorno aluminium alloys (7o75-T6) or engineering plastics (PEEK) via additive manufacturing. This minimises the overall vacuum mass whilst ensuring the necessary mechanical strength. [03] 1 2 Propulsion system and thermal management dynamics [04] The selection and matching of the propulsion system directly determine the endurance and operational reliability of rUAs. For electric propulsion systems. the magnetic flux density, notch fill rate and thermal resistance class of the brushless DCL permanent magnets shall be validated by rigorous dynamometer bench tests: Electronic speed controllers (ESCs) shall have a wheel function
