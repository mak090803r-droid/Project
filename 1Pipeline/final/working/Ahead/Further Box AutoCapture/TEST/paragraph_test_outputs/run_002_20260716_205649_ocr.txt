Offline Inspection at a Remote Energy Facility A maintenance team operates a hybrid-energy station on a dry plateau several hours from the nearest town The station supplies a medical room, a radio repeater, weather instruments, refrigeration. and emergency lighting Solar panels provide most of the daily energy. lithium batteries cover the evening demand, and a compact diesel generator is reserved for long storms. Because winter roads can remain closed for days. the control cabinet stores detailed measurements locally Voltage. current, temperature. fan speed, and vibration are sampled every two hundred milliseconds. Only compressed summaries are transmitted when the radio link becomes available, and medical and communications loads always receive priority During a dust storm on 18 October 2025. solar output fell by thirty-four percent in fifteen minutes Battery-cabinet temperature rose by eight degrees Celsius, and the inverter fan developed an unusual low- frequency vibration The controller did not shut down the entire station. It disconnected secondary heaters. reduced selected lighting. started the backup generator, and issued a maintenance warning. Diagnostic software identified three plausible faults: debris covering part of the solar array, wear in a fan bearing. and increasing contact resistance at a battery connector. These possibilities were ranked. but none was presented as certain because the measurements could be explained by more than one physical condition When the storm weakened. a technician approached the cabinet while wearing a head-mounted camera and an earpiece. Equipment labels contained model numbers, safety notices, alarm codes, and short repair instructions in several languages. The local translation system first checked whether the complete label was visible. It rejected frames with a missing edge. heavy glare. severe perspective distortion, or poor sharpness in the upper region. Four stable frames were required before a photograph was stored If the target was too close, the earpiece asked the technician to step backward.
