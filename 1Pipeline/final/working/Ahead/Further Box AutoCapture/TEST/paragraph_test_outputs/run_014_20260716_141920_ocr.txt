Designing Reliable Automatic Page Capture Automatic page capture seems simple until the camera is worn on the head The user cannot continuously inspect a preview. and small movements alter framing, focus, and perspective. A page that looks acceptable from a distance may have its first lines outside the image or noticeably softer than the lower half. Indoor lighting adds reflections, while daylight produces deep shadows near the page edge. The capture system must therefore decide when a frame is useful and communicate corrections through sound. It should not save the first image containing text, because detection often begins while the wearer is still moving into position The proposed gate combines geometry, focus, exposure, and temporal stability Text boxes are detected in each video frame and merged into a candidate region. The outer region must remain inside a safety margin so no line is cropped. Its width and height must also fall within practical limits: a tiny region provides too few pixels per character, but a region touching the image boundary probably indicates that part of the page is missing Focus is  estimated from Laplacian variance over the full grayscale frame A second measurement covers the upper thirty percent. where slanted paper is commonly out of focus. A capture is rejected when the upper score is much lower than the full-frame score, even if the overall number appears satisfactory One sharp frame is not sufficient evidence. The position and size of the text region must remain stable across four consecutive observations With the selected video interval, this adds roughly eight tenths of a second after the page first appears ready. The delay is short for the wearer but long enough for hand and head motion to settle. Exposure changes are monitored during the same period. If brightness is still drifting. the counter returns to zero When the page is too large, spoken guidance asks the user to step back. Missing space on one side produces a left or right cue, and persistent softness produces a request to hold still or adjust distance.
